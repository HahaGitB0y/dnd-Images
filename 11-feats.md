# Chapter 11: Feats

{{quote
*"Some things you just... know. You've always known. Maybe it's how your brain is wired, maybe it's something you picked up years ago without thinking about it. Either way — it's yours."*
}}

## How Feats Work

Feats represent **innate talents, pre-apocalypse experience, or natural aptitudes** that a character possesses independent of their profession levels. They are not learned through books — they are part of who someone is.

### Gaining Feats

- At character creation, each player selects **1 Feat** (approved by group consensus).
- Additional Feats may be **earned during play** when a character meets the criteria (DM discretion, group agreement).
- A character can have a **maximum of 3 Feats** total.
- Feats must be **justified** — the player should explain why their character has this talent (pre-apocalypse hobby, natural gift, life experience, etc.).
- Basic human functions or things anyone could do **don't count** — a Feat must be something that sets you apart.

## Feat List

### 1. Internal Compass

*You always know which way is north. You rarely get lost and can retrace your steps instinctively.*

- Advantage on all Survival checks for navigation and direction.
- You can retrace any path you've traveled in the last 7 days without a check.
- +2 to Surveyor/Cartographer profession study checks.

---

### 2. Photographic Memory (Partial)

*You remember layouts, faces, and details with unusual clarity. Not perfect — but far better than most.*

- After visiting a location once, you can recall its layout accurately (no check needed for basic mapping).
- Advantage on Intelligence checks to recall information you've seen or read.
- Study sessions grant +10 bonus XP (you retain more from reading).

---

### 3. Tech Literate

*Computers, printers, networks, 3D printers — you grew up with this stuff and it's second nature.*

- IT Specialist profession starts at Level 1 (free, no XP needed).
- Advantage on checks involving computers, printers, and digital fabrication equipment.
- Can operate a 3D printer without any IT Specialist levels (DC 10 INT check).

---

### 4. Maker's Hands

*You've always been the type to take things apart and put them back together. Tinkering is instinct.*

- +2 to all checks involving assembling, disassembling, or modifying mechanical/electronic devices.
- Can attempt basic repairs on any mechanical or electronic item at Level 0 (normally requires Level 1).
- Reduce crafting time by 25% for any hands-on construction.

---

### 5. Iron Liver

*You can drink most people under the table. Your body processes alcohol and toxins faster than normal.*

- Advantage on Constitution saves against alcohol, ingested poisons, and drug side effects.
- You can consume alcohol for morale benefits without the negative effects (no disadvantage the next day).
- +2 to saves against ingested diseases (food poisoning, contaminated water).

\page

### 6. Silver Tongue

*People listen when you talk. You know how to de-escalate, smooth things over, and get what you want without a fight.*

- Advantage on Persuasion checks when meeting new survivors for the first time.
- +2 to all Charisma checks involving bartering and trade.
- Can attempt to calm hostile (non-zombie) NPCs before combat begins (DC 12 CHA).

---

### 7. Light Sleeper

*The slightest sound snaps you awake. You've always been this way.*

- Cannot be surprised while sleeping.
- Advantage on Perception checks while on watch or resting.
- You only need 6 hours for a long rest instead of 8.

---

### 8. Steady Hands

*Your hands don't shake. Not under pressure, not after running, not ever.*

- Advantage on Dexterity checks involving fine motor control (surgery, lockpicking, disarming traps, soldering).
- No penalty to ranged attacks after sprinting or exertion.
- +2 to any crafting check that requires precision.

---

### 9. Pack Mule

*You've always been able to carry more than you should. Broad shoulders, strong back, stubborn will.*

- Carrying capacity increased by 50%.
- No movement penalty when encumbered (up to heavy encumbrance).
- Can carry an unconscious ally at full movement speed.

---

### 10. Nose for Trouble

*Call it a gut feeling. You sense when something is off before anyone else does.*

- Advantage on Initiative rolls.
- +2 to Perception checks to detect ambushes or hidden threats.
- Once per long rest, you can ask the DM: "Does something feel wrong here?" and receive an honest yes/no.

\page

### 11. Green Thumb

*Plants just... grow for you. You've always had a knack for keeping things alive.*

- Advantage on all Farmer and Botanist profession checks at any level.
- Gardens you tend produce 25% more yield.
- +2 to foraging checks even without Botanist levels.

---

### 12. Natural Athlete

*You've always been fit without really trying. You recover faster and push harder than most.*

- +5ft movement speed.
- Recover 1 extra hit die during long rests.
- Advantage on Athletics checks involving running, jumping, and climbing.

---

### 13. Grease Monkey

*Engines make sense to you. Always have. You can hear what's wrong with a machine before you open the hood.*

- Mechanic profession starts at Level 1 (free, no XP needed).
- Advantage on checks to diagnose mechanical problems (identify the issue, not necessarily fix it).
- +2 to all vehicle-related checks (driving, repair, modification).

---

### 14. Combat Awareness

*You've been in enough scraps to know where the next hit is coming from.*

- +2 to AC against opportunity attacks.
- You can use your reaction to grant an adjacent ally +2 AC against one attack.
- Advantage on Perception checks during active combat.

---

### 15. Scavenger's Eye

*You spot useful things that others walk right past. Trash to them — treasure to you.*

- Find 25% more items when looting any location.
- Advantage on Investigation checks when searching containers, rooms, or vehicles.
- Can identify the most valuable item in a room with a DC 10 INT check (instant, no action).

\page

### 16. Quick Study

*You pick things up faster than most. One read-through and it clicks.*

- All study sessions grant +15 bonus XP.
- Study time reduced by 1 hour (minimum 1 hour).
- Can study in less-than-ideal conditions without the normal disadvantage (dim light, moderate noise).

---

### 17. Intimidating Presence

*Something about you makes people hesitate. Size, demeanor, eyes — whatever it is, it works.*

- Advantage on Intimidation checks.
- Hostile NPCs must make a DC 12 WIS save or be unable to attack you first (they pick another target).
- +2 to CHA checks when issuing commands or demands.

---

### 18. Field Medic Instinct

*You don't freeze when you see blood. While others panic, you act.*

- Can attempt to stabilize a dying creature even with zero medical profession levels (DC 15 WIS).
- No disadvantage on Medicine checks while in combat or under stress.
- +2 to First Responder profession study checks.

---

### 19. Night Owl

*You function better in the dark. Your eyes adjust faster and you're sharper after sunset.*

- Darkvision 30ft (dim light appears as normal light).
- Advantage on Stealth checks at night or in darkness.
- No penalty to Perception in dim light.

---

### 20. Hard to Kill

*You've survived things that should have ended you. Your body just doesn't quit.*

- Advantage on death saving throws.
- When you stabilize from 0 HP, regain 1 HP instead of staying at 0.
- +2 to Constitution saves against disease and infection.

\page

### 21. Jury Rigger

*If it's broken, you'll make it work — maybe not pretty, maybe not permanent, but it'll work right now.*

- Can attempt temporary repairs on any broken item regardless of profession level (DC 14, lasts 1d4 hours).
- Improvised tools count as proper tools for you (no improvised tool penalty).
- +2 to all checks involving improvised solutions or workarounds.

---

### 22. Poker Face

*Nobody can read you. Your expression gives nothing away.*

- Advantage on Deception checks.
- Enemies have disadvantage on Insight checks against you.
- Immune to the Intimidation effect of hostile NPCs (you don't scare easy).

---

### 23. Forager's Instinct

*You know what's edible. Something about textures, smells, colours — you just know.*

- Can attempt to identify edible plants and safe water without Botanist levels (DC 12 WIS).
- Advantage on saves against ingested poisons and food-borne illness (you notice before swallowing).
- Foraging yields +1 ration even without profession levels.

---

### 24. Mechanical Sympathy

*You feel how things fit together. Structures, mechanisms, joints — you understand how force flows through them.*

- +2 to Engineer and Carpenter profession checks at any level.
- Can identify structural weak points in buildings (which wall to breach, which support to cut) without Engineer levels (DC 13 INT).
- Advantage on checks to barricade doors and reinforce structures.

---

### 25. Born Leader

*People look to you in a crisis. Not because you asked — because they trust you instinctively.*

- Allies within 15ft gain +1 to saves against fear.
- Once per short rest, you can grant an ally advantage on one check by directing/encouraging them (no action required, must be able to speak).
- +2 to Leader and Military Tactician profession study checks.
